\begin{abstract}
The companion to our analysis of the \emph{Drosophila} central complex
(\texttt{docs/drosophila.tex}) is a vertebrate ring attractor at
comparable anatomical resolution: the GABAergic \rpi{} population of
the larval-zebrafish anterior hindbrain (aHB), whose axons arborise in
the dorsal interpeduncular nucleus (dIPN). \citet{petrucco2023}
demonstrate a sinusoidal activity bump that rotates with the fish's
swims and is otherwise stable over many seconds --- the vertebrate
analogue of the Drosophila E-PG bump, with reciprocal inhibitory
connectivity in dIPN replacing the
$\Delta 7$ / ER6 ring-shaping inhibition of the fly. We port the
connectome-constrained framework of the drosophila document to this
dataset: skeletons, ROI meshes and the (post, pre) adjacency matrix
between the 731 HD-circuit neurons are fetched from the
\emph{neuprint-fish2} server (dataset \texttt{fish2}), and the same
Known-ODE / fully connected / GNN parameterisations of the path-integration
task are reused with a vertebrate Dale convention (all bump cells
inhibitory). This stub document collects the anatomy and connectome
summary; the modelling sections (parallel to \cref{sec:methods} of the
companion) will be filled in once the zebrafish task generator and
training runs are in place.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

In the larval zebrafish, head-direction information is carried by a
population of $\sim 50$--$100$ GABAergic neurons (the \rpi{} cells)
located in rhombomere~1 of the anterior hindbrain \citep{petrucco2023}.
A sinusoidal bump of activity travels around this network in a
direction opposite to the fish's swims, persists for tens of seconds
in the absence of motor activity, and tracks an estimated heading
angle whose magnitude per swim ($\approx \pi/4$~rad) matches the
behaviourally measured turn. The connectivity scheme that supports
the bump --- short-range excitation and long-range, out-of-phase
inhibition --- is identical to the ring-attractor scheme established
for the insect central complex \citep{turner-evans2017,kim2017,green2017},
and Electron-microscopy reconstructions
of the \rpi{} axons show reciprocal inhibitory connectivity in the
dIPN that is in concordance with this picture.

The vertebrate--invertebrate parallel makes the zebrafish dataset the
natural companion to the analysis of \texttt{docs/drosophila.tex}: a
second connectome with a known heading-direction function, derived
under a different vertebrate Dale convention (all inhibitory rather
than mixed E/I), against which the identifiability claims of the
drosophila document can be re-tested.

\section{Anatomy and connectome}
\label{sec:anatomy}

\paragraph{Data.}
Skeletons, ROI meshes and synaptic connections are fetched from
\emph{neuprint-fish2.janelia.org}, dataset \texttt{fish2}.
\texttt{figures/zebrafish/fetch\_zebrafish\_anatomy\_HD.py} writes
SWC skeletons and OBJ meshes to
\texttt{zebrafish\_anatomy\_HD/}; \texttt{fetch\_zebrafish\_connectivity\_HD.py}
writes \texttt{neurons.csv} (731 rows: 443 \texttt{IPNd*} + \texttt{IPNds*}
ring cells, 200 \texttt{RIPN*}, 88 \texttt{pt-IPN*}) and
\texttt{connections.csv} ($14\,391$ directed edges)
to \texttt{zebrafish\_connectome\_HD/}. The loader
\texttt{load\_zebrafish\_hd\_connectome()} in
\texttt{src/connectome\_gnn/generators/connconstr\_data.py} reads
the two CSV files and returns a dictionary with the same canonical
keys as the drosophila loader (\texttt{N}, \texttt{J\_effective},
\texttt{neuron\_types}, \texttt{type\_names}, \texttt{n\_epg},
\texttt{epg\_ix}, \texttt{pen\_subpop\_ix}). The
\texttt{ZebrafishHdTaskRNN} class (registered as \texttt{zebrafish\_hd\_si}
in \texttt{src/connectome\_gnn/models/zebrafish\_hd\_task\_rnn.py}) is a
thin subclass of \texttt{DrosophilaCxTaskRNN} that overrides only the
connectome-loader hook; all dynamics, sign-locking, the 4-scalar
afferent gate, the HD-ring-only readout, and the regulariser machinery
are inherited unchanged.

The semantic remapping inside the loader is:
\begin{itemize}
\item \texttt{n\_epg} = 443: number of dorsal-IPN HD cells
       (\texttt{IPNd*} + \texttt{IPNds*}), \ie{} the \rpi{} ring.
\item \texttt{epg\_ix}: ring ordering of the HD cells, computed from
       the mediolateral soma coordinate \texttt{somaLocationX}
       (Petrucco's Fig.~1g right panel shows the \rpi{} cells lie on an
       anatomical ring along this axis); cells are binned into
       \texttt{n\_glom} = 16 ring positions for downstream decoder
       symmetry with the drosophila side.
\item \texttt{pen\_subpop\_ix} = \texttt{\{PENa\_L, PENa\_R, PENb\_L, PENb\_R\}}
       with \texttt{PENa} populated by \texttt{RIPN*} and \texttt{PENb}
       by \texttt{pt-IPN*}, each split by side from the
       \texttt{instance} suffix; sizes
       $\{102, 101, 41, 44\}$.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Dale convention.}
The \rpi{} cells (\texttt{IPNd*} and \texttt{IPNds*}) are GABAergic
\citep{petrucco2023}; their outgoing weights are sign-flipped and
amplified by 5$\times$ in analogy with the Hulse-2025 treatment of
$\Delta 7$ / ER6 in the fly model. \texttt{RIPN*} and \texttt{pt-IPN*}
have no Dale annotation on the \texttt{fish2} server and are kept
positive (excitatory). The resulting signed matrix is rescaled so
$\max(\mathrm{Re}(\lambda)) = 0.9$, matching the
spectral-radius convention used in the drosophila loader and in the
Beiran \& Litwin-Kumar reference implementation.

\section{Methods}
\label{sec:methods}

\textbf{Recurrent neural network.}\label{method:RNN}
The RNN model is a sign-locked discrete-time recurrent network whose
connectivity is constrained to the larval-zebrafish HD-circuit
connectome (\cref{sec:anatomy}). For each neuron $i \in \{1,\dots,N\}$
with $N = 731$ the subthreshold activity $\hat h_i(t)$ evolves
according to
\begin{equation}
\tau\,\frac{d\hat h_i(t)}{dt}
  = -\hat h_i(t)
  + \sum_j \hat W^{\mathrm{rec}}_{ij}\,\sigma\!\big(\hat h_j(t)\big)
  + \sum_l \hat W^{\mathrm{in}}_{il}\,u_l(t)
  + \hat b_i,
\label{eq:zhd_dynamics}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\hat y(t) = \big(\cos\hat\theta(t),\,\sin\hat\theta(t)\big)
  = \hat W^{\mathrm{out}}\sigma\big(\hat h(t)\big) + \hat b^{\mathrm{out}},
\label{eq:zhd_readout}
\end{equation}
where $\sigma$ is the sigmoid, $\tau = 0.1\,\mathrm{s}$,
$\Delta t = 0.01\,\mathrm{s}$, and the input
$u(t) \in \R^3 = \big(s(t),\,\cos\theta_0\,\delta_{t,0},\,\sin\theta_0\,\delta_{t,0}\big)$
carries a swim-triggered angular impulse $s(t)$ and a one-frame
initial-heading cue. In \petrucco{}~Fig.~3c the network phase advances
by $\approx \pi/4$~rad per swim ($Q_1, Q_3 = 0.49, 1.28$~rad,
$n = 31$~fish), so $s(t)$ is a sparse stream of signed impulses of
typical magnitude $\pi/4$ at swim onsets, taking the role of the
angular-velocity input of the drosophila companion document. Outside
swim events $s(t) = 0$, mirroring the persistent silence of the
\rpi{} bump in non-swimming fish (\petrucco{}~Fig.~3a, c). The
readout from \cref{eq:zhd_readout} reads from the
\texttt{output\_from\_epg\_only} (\ie{} HD-ring-only) block of $N$
neurons unless stated otherwise.

The recurrent matrix is sign-locked to the connectome template
$W^{\mathrm{con}} \in \R^{N \times N}$ exactly as in the
Known-ODE RNN of the drosophila companion document: the trainable
per-edge parameter is the magnitude $\hat S \in \R^{N \times N}$, and
\begin{equation}
\hat W^{\mathrm{rec}}_{ij}
  = |\hat S_{ij}|\,\mathrm{sign}(W^{\mathrm{con}}_{ij})\,(1-\delta_{ij}).
\label{eq:zhd_w_rec}
\end{equation}
$W^{\mathrm{con}}$ comes out of
\texttt{load\_zebrafish\_hd\_connectome} with the Dale convention of
\cref{sec:anatomy}: the outgoing columns of the GABAergic HD-ring
cells (\texttt{IPNd*}, \texttt{IPNds*}) are negated and amplified by
$5\times$, after which the matrix is rescaled to spectral radius
$0.9$. Unlike the fly model, \emph{all} sign-flipped columns belong to
the bump population itself; there is no separate inhibitory cell type
($\Delta 7$, ER6) to swap with the bump.

The swim-triggered drive is anatomically restricted to the
afferent populations through a four-scalar gate on the first column
of $\hat W^{\mathrm{in}}$:
\begin{equation}
\hat W^{\mathrm{in}}_{i,s}
  = v_q \quad \text{if } i \in q,\ \ q \in
   \{\mathrm{RIPN}_L,\mathrm{RIPN}_R,\mathrm{ptIPN}_L,\mathrm{ptIPN}_R\},
\label{eq:zhd_swim_gate}
\end{equation}
with four learnable scalars
$\{v_{\mathrm{RIPN}_L},\,v_{\mathrm{RIPN}_R},\,
  v_{\mathrm{ptIPN}_L},\,v_{\mathrm{ptIPN}_R}\}$
initialised antisymmetrically across hemispheres so that left swims
and right swims drive the bump in opposite directions. The subpopulation
membership comes from \texttt{pen\_subpop\_ix} returned by the loader,
with \texttt{PENa} = \texttt{RIPN*} and \texttt{PENb} = \texttt{pt-IPN*}
(sizes $102 / 101 / 41 / 44$). The two remaining input columns ---
carrying the initial-heading impulse
$(\cos\theta_0,\sin\theta_0)\,\delta_{t,0}$ --- stay free for all
neurons. \texttt{RIPN*} is functionally implicated as the
swim-triggered driver of the bump in \petrucco{}; \texttt{pt-IPN*} is
included in the gate so the optimiser can additionally route motor
efference through the pretectal afferents if that improves heading
recovery (this is the closest zebrafish analogue of the
PEN$_a$/PEN$_b$ split, where the two sub-types are functionally
similar but anatomically distinct).

The learnable parameters are
\begin{equation*}
\big\{\hat S,\;
v_{\mathrm{RIPN}_L},\,v_{\mathrm{RIPN}_R},\,
v_{\mathrm{ptIPN}_L},\,v_{\mathrm{ptIPN}_R},\;
\hat W^{\mathrm{in}}_{:,\theta_0},\,\hat b,\,
\hat W^{\mathrm{out}},\,\hat b^{\mathrm{out}}\big\};
\end{equation*}
the connectome template $W^{\mathrm{con}}$, the
RIPN / pt-IPN subpopulation membership, the neuron-type assignments,
$\tau$ and $\Delta t$ are given. Training optimises a per-frame MSE
on the heading readout with the same two connectome-prior
regularisers and an $\ell_1$ sparsity prior on the recurrent
magnitudes as the drosophila model,
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}
= \tfrac{1}{BT}\sum_{b,t} \|\hat y_{b,t} - y_{b,t}\|_2^2
+ \lambda_{\mathrm{cos}}\,\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{cos}}
+ \lambda_{\mathrm{nf}}\,\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{nf}}
+ \lambda_{1}\,\|\hat S\|_1,
\label{eq:zhd_loss}
\end{equation}
with block losses computed over type-pair masks
$\mathcal{B}_p \subset \{1,\dots,N\}^2$ (one block per ordered
pre/post neuron-type pair $p \in \mathcal{P}$),
\begin{align}
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{cos}}
  &= \tfrac{1}{|\mathcal{P}|}\sum_{p \in \mathcal{P}}
     \!\left[1 - \frac{\langle \hat W^{\mathrm{rec}}_{\mathcal{B}_p},\,
         W^{\mathrm{con}}_{\mathcal{B}_p}\rangle}
       {\|\hat W^{\mathrm{rec}}_{\mathcal{B}_p}\|\,
        \|W^{\mathrm{con}}_{\mathcal{B}_p}\|}\right],
\label{eq:zhd_cos}\\
\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{nf}}
  &= \tfrac{1}{|\mathcal{P}|}\sum_{p \in \mathcal{P}}
     \relu\!\big(\kappa -
       \overline{|\hat W^{\mathrm{rec}}_{\mathcal{B}_p}|}\big)^2.
\label{eq:zhd_nf}
\end{align}
The cosine-distance term $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{cos}}$ anchors each
block to the connectome template; the norm-floor term
$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{nf}}$ prevents any type-pair block from
collapsing to zero by penalising the squared shortfall of the block
mean absolute weight $\overline{|\hat W^{\mathrm{rec}}_{\mathcal{B}_p}|}$
below a fixed floor $\kappa$ (typical value $\kappa = 0.05$). Because
$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{cos}}$ is scale-invariant,
$\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{nf}}$ is required to prevent a degenerate
solution in which the cosine prior is satisfied by an arbitrarily
small recurrent magnitude. The type-pair vocabulary
$\mathcal{P}$ is the cartesian product of the 31 fish2 cell types
identified in \cref{sec:anatomy}, restricted to pairs with at least
one non-zero entry in $W^{\mathrm{con}}$ --- roughly an order of
magnitude more blocks than the fly model, which absorbs the increased
risk of block-level degeneracies into the optimisation. A
linear-reconstruction analysis of the null space of the trained
$\hat W^{\mathrm{rec}}$ --- analogous to the per-cell-type and
per-unit sparsification tests in the appendix of the drosophila
companion document --- is deferred to \cref{sec:results}.

\bigskip
\noindent\textbf{Graph neural network model.}\label{method:GNN}
The GNN model replaces the dense recurrent operator
$\sum_j \hat W^{\mathrm{rec}}_{ij}\,\sigma(\hat h_j)$ of
\cref{eq:zhd_dynamics} with a message-passing graph neural network
(Gilmer \emph{et al.}\ 2017), and --- as in the drosophila companion ---
does \emph{not} hard-code the leak or the firing-rate nonlinearity.
The GNN imposes only the connectome topology of \cref{sec:anatomy}
and the input/output channels of \cref{eq:zhd_dynamics,eq:zhd_readout};
the leak $-\hat h_i$ and the rate non-linearity $\sigma$ must be
\emph{discovered} inside the learned drift. Nodes correspond to
neurons and edges to the non-zero entries of the zebrafish HD
connectome template $W^{\mathrm{con}}$. The GNN learns a latent
embedding $\mathbf a_i \in \R^d$ per neuron, representing compact
cell-type-specific identity; we use $d = 4$ as the agentic-tuning
winner in the drosophila companion document and re-validate it for
the zebrafish data in \cref{sec:results}. Subthreshold activity evolves as
\begin{equation}
\tau\,\frac{d\hat h_i(t)}{dt}
  = f_\theta\!\Big(\hat h_i(t),\,\mathbf a_i,\,
       \sum_{j \in \mathcal N_i}
       \hat W_{ij}\,g_\phi\!\big(\hat h_j(t),\mathbf a_j\big)\Big)
  + \sum_l \hat W^{\mathrm{in}}_{il}\,u_l(t),
\label{eq:zhd_gnn_dynamics}
\end{equation}
with the same readout (\cref{eq:zhd_readout}), swim-gated encoder
(\cref{eq:zhd_swim_gate}), and dynamics constants ($\tau,\Delta t$)
as the RNN. The neighbour set is fixed by the connectome topology,
$\mathcal N_i = \{j : W^{\mathrm{con}}_{ij} \neq 0\}$. The node
update $f_\theta$ and edge message $g_\phi$ are three-layer
perceptrons (width 64, ReLU, linear output). $g_\phi$ maps the
presynaptic state $\hat h_j$ to a per-edge message conditioned on
$\mathbf a_j$ and weighted by the per-edge gain $\hat W_{ij}$;
$f_\theta$ then processes the postsynaptic state $\hat h_i$, the
embedding $\mathbf a_i$, and the aggregated synaptic drive. We
constrain $g_\phi$ to be monotonically increasing in $\hat h_j$ via
the prior $\partial g_\phi / \partial \hat h \ge 0$ (see
\cref{eq:zhd_gnn_loss}); combined with the sign-locked $\hat W_{ij}$
(\cref{eq:zhd_gnn_w}) this restores Dale's law at the per-edge level
without introducing a $\pm$-sign degeneracy. The leak, the
firing-rate non-linearity, and any departure from a linear synapse
must emerge from $(f_\theta, g_\phi, \mathbf a)$ themselves; the
monotonicity priors below provide the minimum inductive bias for
$f_\theta, g_\phi$ to behave like a neuron (postsynaptic drive
increases with net excitatory input). The per-edge weight is
sign-locked to the connectome as in the RNN,
\begin{equation}
\hat W_{ij}
  = |W_{ij}|\,\mathrm{sign}(W^{\mathrm{con}}_{ij})\,(1-\delta_{ij}),
\label{eq:zhd_gnn_w}
\end{equation}
where $W_{ij} \in \R$ is the trainable per-edge magnitude.
In the zebrafish circuit
$\mathrm{sign}(W^{\mathrm{con}}_{ij})$ is negative for every edge
whose presynaptic neuron is in the bump ring (\texttt{IPNd*} or
\texttt{IPNds*}) and positive for every edge from \texttt{RIPN*} or
\texttt{pt-IPN*}; the GNN therefore inherits the inhibitory dIPN
ring directly from the sign pattern, just as the fly GNN inherits
the $\Delta 7$ / ER6 inhibition.

The learnable parameters are
\begin{equation*}
\big\{\theta,\,\phi,\,\mathbf a,\,W,\;
v_{\mathrm{RIPN}_L},\,v_{\mathrm{RIPN}_R},\,
v_{\mathrm{ptIPN}_L},\,v_{\mathrm{ptIPN}_R},\;
\hat W^{\mathrm{in}}_{:,\theta_0},\,\hat b,\,
\hat W^{\mathrm{out}},\,\hat b^{\mathrm{out}}\big\};
\end{equation*}
the connectome template $W^{\mathrm{con}}$, the
RIPN / pt-IPN subpopulation membership, the neuron-type assignments,
$\tau$, $\Delta t$, and the mediolateral ring ordering used by the
decoder are given.

As with the RNN, heading recovery from cos/sin readouts is
fundamentally non-unique: many distinct $(\theta,\phi,\mathbf a,W)$
configurations produce identical readouts. We constrain the solution
space with the same connectome-prior regularisers as the RNN
($\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{cos}}$, $\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{nf}}$ from
\cref{eq:zhd_cos,eq:zhd_nf}, computed on the per-edge gain surface
$\hat W_{ij}$ with floor threshold $\kappa$), together with sparsity
and monotonicity priors on the MLPs:
\begin{align}
\mathcal{L} \;=\;&
  \tfrac{1}{BT}\sum_{b,t}\|\hat y_{b,t} - y_{b,t}\|_2^2
+ \lambda_{\mathrm{cos}}\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{cos}}
+ \lambda_{\mathrm{nf}}\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{nf}} \nonumber\\
& +\;\lambda_0\|\theta\|_1 + \lambda_1\|\phi\|_1
   + \lambda_2\|\theta\| + \lambda_3\|\phi\|
   \;+\;\gamma_0\|W\|_1 + \gamma_1\|W\| \nonumber\\
& +\;\mu_0\!\left\|
       \relu\!\left(+\tfrac{\partial f_\theta(\hat h,\mathbf a,m)}
                          {\partial \hat h}\right)\right\|
   +\;\mu_1\!\left\|
       \relu\!\left(-\tfrac{\partial f_\theta(\hat h,\mathbf a,m)}
                          {\partial m}\right)\right\| \nonumber\\
& +\;\mu_2\!\left\|
       \relu\!\left(-\tfrac{\partial g_\phi(\hat h,\mathbf a)}
                          {\partial \hat h}\right)\right\|
   +\;\mu_3\,\big\|g_\phi(h_\star,\mathbf a)-h_\star\big\|,
\label{eq:zhd_gnn_loss}
\end{align}
where $m = \sum_{j \in \mathcal N_i}\hat W_{ij}\,
g_\phi(\hat h_j,\mathbf a_j)$ is the aggregated synaptic input. The
$\ell_1$ penalties $\lambda_0,\lambda_1$ on the MLP weights bias
$f_\theta, g_\phi$ toward sparser, simpler functions; the $\ell_2$
penalties $\lambda_2,\lambda_3$ prevent weight explosion. The
leak-monotonicity term $\mu_0$ penalises \emph{positive} slopes of
$f_\theta$ in the postsynaptic state $\hat h$, encoding the prior
that the drift contains a leak
($\partial f_\theta / \partial \hat h \le 0$); $\mu_1$ penalises
negative slopes of $f_\theta$ in the aggregated input $m$, encoding
the prior that postsynaptic drive increases with net excitatory
input. Together, $\mu_0$ and $\mu_1$ force the discovered drift to be
decreasing in self-voltage and increasing in synaptic input --- the
two qualitative signatures of \cref{eq:zhd_dynamics}. The term $\mu_2$
enforces the analogous monotonicity prior on $g_\phi$ in the
presynaptic voltage. The normalisation term $\mu_3$ anchors $g_\phi$
at a reference subthreshold value $h_\star$, removing the
multiplicative degeneracy between $g_\phi$ and $\hat W$ in
\cref{eq:zhd_gnn_dynamics}. Hyperparameters
$\lambda_{\{0,1,2,3\}},\,\gamma_{\{0,1\}},\,\mu_{\{0,1,2,3\}}$ and the
MLP widths/depths are tuned with the same agentic optimisation
procedure as the drosophila companion, with $r_{\mathrm{roll},1k}$ on
a deterministic swim-impulse rollout (analogous to the
constant-$\omega$ rollout of the fly model) as the primary score.

\paragraph{What the GNN can and cannot identify in the zebrafish
preparation.} The fly preparation is unusually informative for
identifiability because $\Delta 7$ and ER6 are the \emph{two} clearly
inhibitory cell types, with nested postsynaptic targets, so the GNN
can swap one for the other and still satisfy the path-integration
input--output map (drosophila companion document, GNN-result
discussion). The zebrafish dIPN ring is all-inhibitory: there is no
analogous E/I swap to test. What remains testable is the structurally
similar question \emph{within} the inhibitory pool --- whether HD
information can be reallocated among the \texttt{IPNd13*},
\texttt{IPNd17*} and \texttt{IPNds*} sub-families without breaking
the readout. The per-cell-type mutual-information analysis of the
drosophila document ports unchanged and is the natural tool for that
test.
